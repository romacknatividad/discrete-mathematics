Discrete Mathematics Books

A curated reading shelf for undergraduate study and deeper follow-up

Back to course page

This is a curated list rather than an exhaustive bibliography. The goal is to collect books that are either widely used in discrete mathematics courses or especially useful for proof, graph theory, combinatorics, and theoretical computer science.

How to Use This Shelf

Start with a core textbook, add one proof-focused book, and then branch into graph theory, combinatorics, or advanced theory as needed.

Best First Textbook

Susanna S. Epp is usually the easiest starting point if you want clarity in logic and proof writing.

Best Broad Reference

Kenneth H. Rosen is the standard large survey text used in many computer science programs.

Best Stretch Book

Concrete Mathematics is excellent once basic proof and counting ideas already feel comfortable.

Core Textbooks

These are the books most students should look at first.

Core

Discrete Mathematics with Applications

Susanna S. Epp

Clear, careful, and especially strong on logic, sets, relations, and proofs. A strong choice for first exposure.

Best for: students who want a readable introduction with careful reasoning.

Publisher page

Core

Discrete Mathematics and Its Applications

Kenneth H. Rosen

A broad standard textbook covering logic, proofs, counting, graphs, relations, number theory, and applications to computing.

Best for: students who want one comprehensive reference for a full course.

Review page

Core

Discrete Mathematics with Graph Theory

Edgar G. Goodaire and Michael M. Parmenter

A friendly survey text that balances proof techniques with strong graph-theory coverage.

Best for: students who want graph theory to play a bigger role in an introductory course.

Book page

Proof and Logic Books

Useful if the main difficulty is learning how to read and write proofs.

Proof

Book of Proof

Richard Hammack

A popular free text focused on the language of mathematics and the standard proof methods students need early.

Best for: self-study and proof practice before or during a discrete mathematics course.

Open textbook page

Proof

How to Prove It

Daniel J. Velleman

A classic proof-transition book often recommended to students learning formal reasoning for the first time.

Best for: students who need a dedicated proof book alongside the course textbook.

Catalog search

Graph Theory and Combinatorics

Useful for students who want stronger coverage beyond the standard introductory chapter treatment.

Graph

Introduction to Graph Theory

Douglas B. West

A respected graph theory textbook with serious depth and a strong reputation among graph theorists.

Best for: a dedicated graph theory course after introductory discrete mathematics.

Publisher page

Comb

Applied Combinatorics

Alan Tucker

Strong on counting methods, graph models, recurrence ideas, and problem-solving with discrete structures.

Best for: students who want more worked combinatorics and applications.

Publisher page

Advanced and Famous Follow-Up Books

These are especially influential once the fundamentals already feel comfortable.

Advanced

Concrete Mathematics: A Foundation for Computer Science

Ronald L. Graham, Donald E. Knuth, and Oren Patashnik

A famous and demanding text connecting recurrence relations, sums, generating functions, number theory, and algorithmic mathematics.

Best for: stronger students interested in algorithms and deeper combinatorial methods.

Author page

Advanced

Introduction to Lattices and Order

B. A. Davey and H. A. Priestley

A classic introduction to ordered structures, lattices, and algebraic viewpoints that appear in logic and computer science.

Best for: students who want to go beyond standard undergraduate survey material.

Reference page

Advanced

An Introduction to the Theory of Numbers

G. H. Hardy and E. M. Wright

A famous number theory classic. Not a general discrete math text, but highly useful for the number theory side of the subject.

Best for: students who become especially interested in divisibility, primes, and modular arithmetic.
