Course Description

The course emphasizes correct mathematical reasoning and its application to computing. Students learn how to construct proofs, interpret formal statements, model discrete structures, and analyze the mathematical behavior of algorithms and systems.

Learning Outcomes

  • Construct proofs using direct argument, contradiction, contrapositive, cases, and induction.
  • Use sets, functions, relations, and graphs to model computational problems.
  • Solve counting problems and analyze recursive definitions and recurrence relations.
  • Explain how discrete mathematics supports algorithmic and formal reasoning.

  • Divisibility, primes, and modular arithmetic

Main Subtopics
